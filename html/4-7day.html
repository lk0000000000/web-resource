4-7 Day Trip

A four to seven day trip’s worth of clothing ideally fits into a backpack! If you have access to a laundry machine, it really reduces the amount of clothes you will need to bring with you. This depends on a few factors though. If you are going to be staying in different places, it will not be as practical to wait for your laundry to finish drying overnight (not every place will have a dryer!). If you will be able to use the laundry machine to your full advantage, pack your clothes as though you will be doing laundry every third day. This means that you can ideally pack a maximum of two shirts (with a third one worn on your person). But for a trip that’s almost a week, you would probably want variety, so throw in one extra shirt.
Obviously you also want to take your bathing things and your essential technology, so throw that into the mix as well. Headphones and a downloaded book are always a necessity in my opinion because of all the travel time to and from your various destinations.
Here’s a sample list (if you have laundry access):

  • 4 pairs of socks
  • 4 pairs of underwear
  • 3 shirts
  • 1 pair of pants or skirt
  • Toiletry bag containing a toothbrush, toothpaste, floss, deodorant, bar soap
  • Wallet, phone, keys
  • Headphones
  • Charger
This list doesn’t include what you will be wearing on your person, so overall you would have one more copy of most of your clothing staples. Note on choice of bottoms: if you will be wearing a type of bottom, try to think about the weather and pack something a little bit different for the extra thing you will be bringing (jeans and shorts, for example, or thicker pants and thinner pants). For colder regions you will still have to bring something corresponding to the temperature, but you can get creative with styling: different types of long pants, or a long skirt and fleece tights combo. This will keep your limited trip wardrobe varied enough for any slight variation in weather during your stay.
If you will not have access to laundry at all on your trip, you won’t have to change much from my initial list aside from more socks and underwear. I am a strong believer of wearing shirts at least twice as they really do not get stinky after one wear. But if you are going to be visiting a really hot climate, I would pack another shirt. Worst case scenario, socks and underwear could be washed in a sink with the bar soap you will be bringing, but if you do not want to deal with drying time and dubious cleansing, bring a pair of each for every day of your stay.
This list doesn’t include any trip-specific necessities like formal wear, so pack anything extra accordingly.
